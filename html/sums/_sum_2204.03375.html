They propose a new evaluation metric named Flexible Goal Accuracy (FGA) They show that FGA is a better metric of DST model performance. FGA considers the performance of both cumulative and turn-level prediction and provides a better insight than the exist-uponing metrics used for DST models. They also show that FlexibleGoal Accuracy is a more reliable metric than Joint Goal Accuracy.
Improving JGA can sometimes degrade performance of predicting Tt mainly due to the presence of annotation inconsistencies in the avail-uablyable datasets. To provide a fair esti-icatingmate, it requires not only track the performance of the cumulative belief state but also turn-level belief state as well. The central idea of FGA is that it tracks performance of DST with FGA instead of JGA. FGA has been proposed as a novel evaluation metric for DST called "Flexible Goal Accuracy"

The main contributions of the work are as follows 1: Detailed analysis of the existing DST metrics. Proposal of Flexible Goal Accuracy (FGA) can keep track of both joint and turn-level performances simultaneously. FGA is to partially penalize a misprediction which is locally correct i.e. the source of the mispredic-tion is some earlier turn. The main contributor of the work is as follows: Detailing analysis of existing metrics and FGA performance on MultiWOZ dataset.
