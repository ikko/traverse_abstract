DynLight: Realize dynamic phase duration with multi-level trafﬁc signal control frame-work, DynLight. DynLight uses an optimization method Max-QueueLength (M-QL) to determine phase and uses a deep-Q-network to determine the corresponding duration. They further propose DynLight-C that adopts a well trained deep-trained deep Q-network of DynLight and replace M-QL by a cyclical control policy that actuate a set of phases in order to realize cyclical phase structure.
Traditional methods for TSC rely on hand-crafted trafﬁc signals. Drivers prefer a cyclical phase structure for traf-centric signals. They develop a multi-level signal control framework DynLight that determines the phase order in the first stage. DynLight can be replaced to realize cyclical-phase actuation to support real-world deployment. With DynLight well trained, the phase selection policy can be. replaced to realized cyclical.phase actuations to support Real-World deployment.

Most RL methods use ﬁxed action interval(tduration) and determine phase every tduration. The duration of each phase can only be multiple times the length of the tduration and can’t realize dynamic duration. RL methods cannot work when the phase order is re-quired in a determined order, these methods can't work because they can determine arbitrary phase. They consider multi-intersection TSC, in which each intersection is controlled by an RL agent. Agent i views the environments as its observation and takes an action to control the signal of intersection i. The goal of the agent i is to take an optimal action to maximize its cumulative reward.

They train DynLight with cyclical phase selection as DynLight-C. They use two groups (JiNan and HangZhou) of datasets that describe how vehicles travel across the network.
