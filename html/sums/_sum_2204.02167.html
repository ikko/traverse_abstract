Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) are the topmost cause of death globally, leading to an estimated 16% of total deaths annually. Atherosclerosis, a narrowing or blocking of arteries due to a plaque buildup, is one of the most common CVDs. In-stent restenosis (ISR) is a condition that leads to an excessive amount of neointima formation and a repeat narrowing of the vessel. To study the ISR process, a three-dimensional computational model, ISR3D, has been developed to simulate the process.

An eﬃcient data-driven surrogate model based on Gaus-gian process regression was developed to substitute the original model for the evaluations in the inverse uncertainty quantiﬁcation. The vessel wall is modelled as a centre-based agent-based (AB) model, where individual cells are modelled like spheres, interacting via adhesive and repulsive forces. Bayesian IUQ requires thousands of runs of expensive computer simulations. The paper is arranged as follow.

Bayesian calibration relates prior information with uncertainty to pos-terior information based on the likelihood of simulated outputs from the model. In each iteration, the pos-centric probability density functions of calibration parameters are updated in a way that is most likely to align with the benchmark data. The posterior-centric distributions of the parameters inferred after a suﬃcient number of itera-typicallytions are the most probable calibration of the model, which means that a model calibrated with a Bayesian model can eﬁciently produce expected behavithe by determining the best estimate of parameter uncertainties.

They introduce a zero-mean Gaussian bias term that accounts for uncertainty sources, and its variance is calibrated throughout the IUQ process. The AB model of arterial tissue is implemented using OpenMP, which supports shared-memory, parallel multiprocessing programming. The average simulation time of a single uniaxial strain test ranges between 1.3 to 1.5 minutes wall clock. The GP model takes 0.2 milliseconds on average for producing the stress prediction irrespective of the magnitude of input coeﬃcient values. A single calibration iteration of the IUQ for one of three arterial layers would take 96 hours if running the original model.

The benchmark data every IUQ and calibration problem relies on was obtained analytically by following an isotropic material model of arterial wall Separate surrogate models for intima, media and media were obtained. Bayesian calibration with a bias term correction was performed as a technique of IUQ to quantify uncertainties of attractive force coeﬃcients and estimate their values based on the analytical data. As a result, uncertainties about the unknown parameters were reduced, and the attractive forces of the current force function were formed.

This paper applied inverse uncertainty quantiﬁcation to determine inter-action forces in the cell-resolved AB arterial tissue model from the exper-imental data of biological tissue’s macroscopic behavithe. ABM arterial. tissue layers were determined, providing a reasonable macro-.scopic stress-strain relationship for uniaxial strain tests. Ultimately, these models will help us to understand and treat cardiac pathologies. The methodology presented here can be applied to other microscale models where mechanical properties are important for osteopathology.
